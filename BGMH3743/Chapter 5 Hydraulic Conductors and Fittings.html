Chapter 5 Hydraulic Conductors and Fittings

Hydraulic systems use primary four types of conductors:

  1. Steel pipes
  2. Steel tubing
  3. Plastic tubing
  4. Flexible hose

The choice of which type of conductor to use depends primarily on the systems operating pressure and flow rate.

In addition, the selection depends on environmental conditions such as the type of fluid, operating temperatures, vibration and relative motion between connected components.

A conductor must have a large enough cross-sectional area to handle the flow rate requirement without producing excessive fluid velocity.

The average velocity is defined as the volume flow rate divided by the pipe cross-sectional area

Working pressure

We have discussed the burst pressure of a pipe, however, this does not take into account impurities, pressure spikes, cracks, and damage that will cause the pipe to fail. A factor of safety, FS must be introduced to account for these effects. A rule of thumb of FS for various operating pressures is given below:

P = 0 1000 psi. FS = 8
P = 1000 2500 psi. FS = 6
P > 2500 psi. FS = 4
